{% translate "About" %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} The urban development scenario simulator is a tool that estimates the expected environmental, social, and economic impacts of implementing public policies in urban development, mobility, and the environment. Its objective is to test various scenarios of urban expansion, population density, land use, public transport routes, creation of employment-attracting areas, as well as changes in water and energy consumption, and improvements in transport technologies; to demonstrate their benefits or implications through various metrics that contribute to decision-making and to raising awareness among the population about the impacts of cities, their dynamics and the possibilities to improve their sustainability. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %}How does this tool work?{% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate trimmed %} The Scenario Simulator is a web-based tool that helps stakeholders assess the municipality's plans. It uses a set of sustainability indicators to compare a city's current performance against different scenarios. These scenarios are created by combining several policy interventions, called policy levers. {% endblocktranslate %}
{% blocktranslate %}The simulator can facilitate the following activities:{% endblocktranslate %}

  • {% blocktranslate %}Analyze current performance: Identify strengths, weaknesses, and areas for improvement.{% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate trimmed %}Test different scenarios: See how various combinations of policies (e.g., infrastructure, regulations) impact sustainability in the future. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate trimmed %}Make data-driven decisions: Choose the combination of policies that best advance toward sustainable development.{% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} The simulator is an IMPLANG initiative. Its main goal is to support consensus building and integrated planning to encourage the construction of a brighter and more sustainable future.
The simulator results from a joint effort between IMPLANG, the Mario Molina Center, CAPSUS, and UPTech. {% endblocktranslate %}
